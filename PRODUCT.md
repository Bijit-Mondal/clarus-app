# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Teams and individuals using a SaaS business application — people who sign in, manage accounts, configure settings, and work through core product workflows. They use Clarus during focused work sessions: checking status, taking action, and moving tasks forward without friction or visual noise.

## Product Purpose

Clarus App is a SaaS business application. The near-term goal is a polished app shell — navigation, layout, design system, and placeholder views — that establishes a confident, scalable foundation before feature depth. Success looks like a coherent product frame where every screen feels intentional, readable, and ready for real workflows to land.

## Brand Personality

Confident · Direct · Capable

Voice is assured and plain-spoken: say what matters, skip filler, and let structure carry authority. The name *Clarus* (clarity) is the through-line — users should always know where they are, what they can do, and what happened.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS scaffolding: cream backgrounds, gradient heroes, hero metrics, eyebrow kickers on every section, identical icon-card grids.
- Over-designed chrome: glassmorphism, paired border-plus-wide-shadow "ghost cards," oversized radii, decorative motion.
- Enterprise clutter: dense tables with tiny gray text, cramped layouts, no breathing room.
- Overly playful UI: sketchy illustrations, bouncy elastic motion, loud decorative color for its own sake.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity is the product.** Hierarchy, labels, and layout should answer "where am I?" and "what's next?" before the user has to think.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** Strong structure and typography over decoration; every visual element earns its place.
3. **Design serves the workflow.** Screens exist to move work forward — navigation, states, and density follow the task, not a template.
4. **Foundation before features.** The shell and design system must scale; placeholder views should feel shippable, not throwaway.
5. **Reject category reflex.** Avoid default SaaS tropes; if it could belong to any product in the category, rework it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: sufficient color contrast (including placeholders and muted text), visible focus states, keyboard-operable controls, and semantic structure. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animation. Use color plus non-color cues for status and data visualization.
